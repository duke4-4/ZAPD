Who We Serve

ZAPD supports oral health for infants, children, adolescents, and patients with special needs. We connect families with trusted guidance and connect clinicians with evidence-based pediatric dentistry resources.

Whether you are a parent seeking preventive advice or a dental professional expanding your practice, our association is built around healthy smiles for every stage of childhood.

Community Impact

Through school visits, rural outreach camps, and public education campaigns, ZAPD members work to reduce untreated dental disease among Zimbabwe’s youth.

We partner with local health teams to deliver screenings, fluoride programs, and parent workshops—bringing care closer to the communities that need it most.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should my child first see a dentist?

We recommend that a child's first dental visit should be within six months after the first tooth erupts or by their first birthday, whichever comes first.

How can I prepare my child for their first dental visit?

Talk to them positively about the dentist, read books about visiting the dentist, and avoid using words like "pain" or "hurt". Make it sound like a fun adventure!

Are dental X-rays safe for children?

Yes, pediatric dental X-rays are very safe. The amount of radiation is extremely low, and we use lead aprons to ensure your child is protected.

What should I do if my child knocks out a permanent tooth?

Find the tooth, hold it by the crown (not the root), gently rinse it if dirty, and try to reinsert it. If that's not possible, put it in milk and see a dentist immediately.
