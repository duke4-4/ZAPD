What We Do

Elevating Paediatric Dental Care

The South African Association of Paediatric Dentistry (SAAPD) is a non-profit organization with the ultimate goal of promoting optimal oral health for our Nation's children. Paediatric dentistry is challenging both clinically and emotionally. Most of the time, it is also interesting, rewarding, and very entertaining!

Continuing Professional Development

We are committed to advancing the professional growth of paediatric dentists through superior continuing education—hosting courses, seminars, and congresses that showcase the latest in dental knowledge and practice.

Public Awareness

We distribute educational materials to parents, caregivers, and the public, highlighting the importance of early dental care and promoting preventive practices from infancy.

Research and Development

We promote continuous education and research in paediatric dentistry, providing professionals with the latest insights on advanced treatment modalities and materials.

Advocacy for Specialisation

One of our core ambitions is to have paediatric dentistry recognised as a specialty by the Health Professions Council of South Africa, ensuring a specialised curriculum at academic institutions that reflects the unique needs of children's dental care.

Community Outreach

SAAPD proudly facilitates outreach projects, ensuring children in less accessible communities receive the essential dental care and check-ups they need for optimal oral health.

Pursuit of Excellence

SAAPD is dedicated to stimulating enthusiasm and commitment among colleagues to continually strive for excellence, ensuring every young patient receives the best possible dental care.

International Collaboration

Seeking affiliation with similar international organisations allows us to foster a global exchange of ideas and best practices in paediatric dental care, for the benefit of children everywhere.

Medical Aid Inclusion

The SAAPD is actively campaigning for the introduction and adoption of paediatric-specific dentistry codes by South African medical aid companies, aiming to secure better health coverage for the nation's youth.
